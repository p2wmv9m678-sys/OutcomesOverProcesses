The Argument

A simple inversion, quietly fatal

Too many teams fall in love with the activity of producing something and lose sight of the result the customer actually wanted. The process exists to serve the outcome — but somewhere along the way the order flips, and the process becomes the goal it was only ever meant to serve.

Drawing on case studies, history, and the shift now underway in the AI era, Outcomes Over Processes shows why the organizations that endure hold the outcome fixed and let the process flex — and gives leaders a practical way to do the same.

About the Author

Sean W. Chambers

Sean W. Chambers is an operations and pipeline leader, a U.S. Army veteran, and the author of Outcomes Over Processes. Years of midstream and operations leadership left him with a set of questions about why some organizations focus on what they produce for the customer while others fixate on the activity of producing it.

He earned his MBA with a minor in Data Analytics from Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi, and is a Doctor of Business Administration candidate (ABD) at William Howard Taft University. Every factual claim in the book is independently verified against primary sources. He writes from Midland, Texas.
